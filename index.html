Latest Info on Air Filters

Air filters are an integral, but more often under-appreciated component of a principal HVAC system. They are designed to filter out bits of pollen and dust which are believed to circulate around the home and causes the air quality indoors to be poor. Air filters also act as the first line of defense against bigger objects. One example of these are pieces of loose insulation which are pulled into the system where they could pose a fire risk or cause damage. 

Changing air filters regularly is important not only for preventing a function failure of your HVAC system but also for your own personal benefits. Understand that every bit of air that is handled by the HVAC system has to pass through the air filter one way or another. When this happens, the filter catches different kinds of particles that pollute your home such as fabric fibers, dust, pet dander, mold and fungal pollen. Over time, this causes the fine mesh which is where the air passes through to become denser. When you don't change your air filter regularly, it makes the air unable to pass through readily.   

Here are the top four reasons why you need to change your air filter regularly.

1. It improves air quality.

Changing your air filter regularly helps in improving the air quality in your home. Imagine a home that's free from allergens in the air. If you have allergies, air filters can minimize irritations which is highly beneficial in such cases.   

2. It makes your house cleaner. 

Reduce the volume of dust and hair on your floors, shelves, and more by changing your air filter regularly. As more dust gets caught in your air filter, there's less of it that stays in your house. This means you spend less time and energy when you need to clean the house. Furthermore, dusting and using a vacuum frequently causes less buildup in your air filters. 

3. It improves your health.

Having indoor air that's cleaner and free from impurities can help improve asthma and allergies. If you experience certain conditions such as itchy nose, difficulty breathing, and congestion, you may opt to change your air filters more often. You can also consider installing a home filtration system.

4. It improves the efficiency of your HVAC system.

When you change your air filter regularly, your HVAC system becomes more efficient in its performance. It helps in making the system to last longer while decreasing its need for maintenance. Moreover, it allows you to save money on your energy bill.

Frequently Asked Question

What does an air filter do?

Air filters used in your engine protects it from damage brought by dirt, sand, dust, and other harmful contaminants.

Do you really need an air filter?

Air filters are important for everyone, particularly if you're suffering from certain conditions such as asthma and hay fever or if you have allergies.

Do air filters really help?

Study shows that using air filters can definitely help in cleaning indoor air by removing harmful particles such as smoke, mold, and allergens.

What is the best type of air filter for home?

HEPA (High-efficiency particulate air) filters are said to be one of the most efficient types of HVAC filters. They are known to have the ability to remove allergens and air-borne pollutants in the house by up to 99.97 percent.

What happens if you don't change your air filter?

Not changing your air filter can affect its performance in keeping dust and other debris from circulating through the HVAC system.
